Who We Are

An institution built on faith, service & scholarship

For over seven decades, Ihlamudheen Madrasa has been a trusted home of Islamic learning, shaping generations with knowledge that nurtures both the heart and the mind.

Our Mission

To provide authentic Islamic education that builds strong faith, upright character, and sincere devotion, empowering students to serve Allah and their community.

Our Vision

To be a beacon of Qur'anic light and moral excellence — raising confident, God-conscious learners who carry Islamic values into every walk of life.

Our Values

Sincerity (Ikhlas), knowledge ('Ilm), discipline (Adab), compassion (Rahma), and service (Khidma) shape everything we teach and every child we nurture.

Our Story

Founded in 1954

Founded in 1954, Ihlamudheen Madrasa began as a small circle of learners gathered around dedicated teachers with a single purpose: to preserve and pass on the timeless teachings of Islam.

From those humble beginnings, the madrasa has grown into a thriving institution serving hundreds of families — yet our purpose remains unchanged. We continue to teach the Qur'an, the Sunnah, and sound Islamic values with the same sincerity and devotion that inspired our founders.

Today, generations of students who first learned their alphabet and their prayers within these walls return to enroll their own children — a living testament to the trust of the community.

Knowledge without character is incomplete. Here, we teach the Qur'an to the tongue, its meaning to the mind, and its beauty to the heart — so that every child grows in both faith and virtue.

What We Teach

Programs for every stage of learning

A structured curriculum that grows with your child — from the first letters of the Qur'an to deep understanding of the deen.

Qur'an & Tajweed

Correct recitation from the Noorani Qa'idah through fluent reading, with the rules of Tajweed taught step by step.

Hifz al-Qur'an

A dedicated memorization program guiding committed students to become huffaz, with regular revision and care.

Islamic Studies

Aqidah, Fiqh, Seerah and Hadith taught at an age-appropriate level to build a firm foundation of belief and practice.

Arabic Language

Reading, writing and conversational Arabic that opens the door to understanding the Qur'an in its own words.

Akhlaq & Adab

Islamic manners, ethics and etiquette — honesty, respect and kindness — woven into daily learning and conduct.

Kindergarten Deen

A gentle, joyful introduction for our youngest learners — short surahs, du'as, and love for Allah and His Messenger.
